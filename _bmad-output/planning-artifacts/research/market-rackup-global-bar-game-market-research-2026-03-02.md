## Customer Insights

### The Competitive Socializing Mega-Trend

The single most important macro-finding for RackUp. **Competitive socializing** — technology-enhanced social games in buzzy venues with food and drink — is reshaping how young adults spend their leisure time.

- **93% of Gen Z** have participated in competitive socializing activities, vs just 60% of Baby Boomers
- Overall participation has **jumped 13 percentage points** between 2019–2023, with ~70% of all consumers now participating
- **64% of Gen Z** played a social entertainment game in the last month — now *exceeding* the 60% who went to a pub just for a drink
- **4 in 5 Gen Z** and **2 in 3 millennials** would be more likely to visit pubs offering games, themed events, and interactive experiences

_Sources: [Mintel Gen Z Competitive Socializing Study](https://www.mintel.com/press-centre/gen-zs-now-prefer-to-play-social-entertainment-games-than-go-for-a-drink-in-the-pub/), [The Round Table Group](https://trt.group/news/a-competitive-socializing-revolution-millennials-and-gen-z-embrace-the-new-wave/), [CGA Strategy](https://cgastrategy.com/gen-z-high-tempo-pub-culture/)_

**What this means for RackUp:** You're riding a tidal wave, not swimming upstream. The market is actively moving toward exactly what RackUp offers.

### Pool Culture: The Critical Global Split

#### Western Markets (US, UK, Australia) — Pool = Social Bar Activity [High Confidence]

- In the US, pool is "virtually synonymous with bar culture" — tables in homes, bars, and rec centers. The APA (American Poolplayers Association) counts millions of amateur members
- In the UK, pub-style pool enjoys broad casual appeal alongside formal snooker culture
- North America holds **38.5% of the global pool tables market** revenue share, driven by strong recreational gaming culture
- Multi-activity centres captured 24% of adult participation, with non-organised casual play accounting for 27%
- Pool tables are "becoming fixtures in trendy bars, social clubs" as part of the competitive socializing trend

_Sources: [Black Label Billiards](https://blacklabelbilliards.com/blogs/blog/where-is-billiards-most-popular), [Pool Table Portfolio](https://pooltableportfolio.com/blogs/magazine/the-rise-and-fall-of-pool-halls-a-social-and-cultural-shift), [Data Bridge Market Research](https://www.databridgemarketresearch.com/reports/global-pool-tables-market), [TableSkills.com](https://www.tableskills.com/pool-billiards-2025-trends-tournaments-innovations/)_

#### Vietnam & Southeast Asia — Billiards = Serious Sport (with stigma baggage) [High Confidence]

- Vietnam plays **carom billiards (3-cushion)** primarily — a precision discipline with tables that **literally have no pockets**, making pocket-pool game mechanics irrelevant
- Billiards in Vietnam historically carried stigma: "association with alcohol, gambling, and prostitution" — the sport is now actively *distancing itself* from that image
- Young Vietnamese (18–30) are embracing billiards as a **legitimate competitive sport**, with frequent tournaments "focused on interaction and learning rather than winning or losing"
- Vietnam is building a reputation as a "competitive and capable cue sport country" on the world stage
- Facebook communities like "Cong Dong Bida – Chia Se Dam Me" focus on **skill sharing and passion**, not party culture
- Billiards halls serve **tea and coffee**, not beer — drinking and billiards are physically separate venues
- Vietnam's actual party culture centers on **karaoke (KTV)**, not bar games

_Sources: [Tuoi Tre News](https://news.tuoitre.vn/in-ho-chi-minh-city-more-and-more-young-people-fond-of-billiards-despite-prejudice-10376982.htm), [Saigoneer](https://saigoneer.com/vietnam-sports/27996-adding-billiards-to-saigon-s-pantheon-of-pastimes), [Vietnam News](https://vietnamnews.vn/life-style/expat-corner/1661183/viet-nam-s-favourite-game.html), [AzBilliards Forums](https://forums.azbilliards.com/threads/vietnamese-billiards.25287/)_

**Verdict on the Vietnam feedback: VALIDATED.** Vietnamese billiards culture is moving toward seriousness and respectability — layering party/drinking chaos on top would run against the cultural current. The feedback was accurate.

#### The Vietnam Paradox [High Confidence]

Ironically, while Vietnamese billiards culture doesn't fit RackUp's party concept, Vietnam's **drinking culture** is enormous:

- Vietnam ranks **7th in the world** for beer consumption, ahead of the UK
- Average Vietnamese person (15+) consumes **~170 liters of beer per year**
- "Nhậu" culture (social drinking gatherings) is deeply embedded — beer is "the ultimate social lubricant"
- Bia hơi (draft beer halls) are massively social

**The disconnect: Vietnamese people drink heavily AND play billiards — but not at the same time, and increasingly not in the same place.** Drinking happens at bia hơi stalls and restaurants; billiards happens at dedicated halls/cafes.

_Sources: [PMC - Alcohol and Drinking Cultures in Vietnam](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4725306/), [VL Studies - Nhậu Culture](https://vlstudies.com/5-amazing-insights-into-vietnamese-nhau-culture/), [The Shiv - Vietnam Beer Industry](https://the-shiv.com/vietnam-beer/), [Khiri Travel](https://khiri.com/explore-vietnamese-beer-culture/)_

#### Southeast Asia Bright Spot: The Philippines [Medium Confidence]

The Philippines may be RackUp's best SEA market — pocket billiards (not carom) is the dominant format, deeply embedded in bar and social culture, English is widely spoken, and the party-oriented social culture aligns with RackUp's chaos/fun framing.

### Answer to Core Research Questions

**Q1: Does pool culture fit party gaming overlays?**
**Yes — in Western markets.** In the US, UK, and Australia, pool is firmly a casual, social, bar-embedded activity. The culture already associates pool with drinking, socializing, and fun. RackUp enhances what's already there. In Vietnam and much of Asia, billiards (especially carom) is moving toward serious competitive sport — the overlay would fight the cultural current.

**Q2: Is the Vietnam feedback accurate?**
**Yes — validated with high confidence.** Vietnamese billiards culture features carom tables (no pockets), dedicated halls serving tea/coffee (not bars), and a community actively distancing from alcohol/gambling stigma. Drinking culture is massive in Vietnam but exists in separate venues (bia hơi stalls, karaoke). The two don't mix.

**Q3: Which 2-3 markets are most promising?**
1. **United States** — Largest bar pool culture, cornhole (#1 rec sport), beer pong, massive party app spending, 38.5% of global pool market revenue

**Q4: Global-first or market-specific launch?**
**Global-first with English is the right call**, but with a strategic priority order:
- **Phase 1 (Launch):** English-language global release. Primary focus: US, UK, Australia, Canada

- **Vietnam:** Deprioritize as a target market. Vietnamese users who want it can still use the global version, but don't design for this market

**Q5: What made comparable apps succeed across cultures?**
- **Jackbox:** Deep localization (local comedic writers, region-specific content), not just translation
- **Among Us:** Language-light mechanics (minimal text required), visual communication, streamer virality
- **Kahoot:** Universal competitive loop (action → result → leaderboard), works across cultures because competition is universal
- **Flight Club:** Highlight reel sharing creates free viral marketing across social media

**RackUp's advantage:** The core mechanics (make shot → result → leaderboard → items → punishments) are largely language-light and culturally universal. Punishments and missions need localization, but the game engine works globally.
