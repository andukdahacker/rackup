RACKUP - Turn Bar Games Into Absolute Chaos
=============================================

Think Kahoot meets Mario Kart... but for pool night.

THE IDEA
--------
RackUp is a party app that turns a regular game of pool into
a hilarious, competitive, unforgettable experience. Everyone
joins a room on their phone. You play real pool, but the app
layers on missions, power-ups, punishments, and pure chaos.

HOW IT WORKS
------------
1. One person creates a room, everyone else joins with a code
2. You play pool like normal, but after each shot the app:
   - Assigns secret missions ("bank shot for double points")
   - Drops Mario Kart-style items (blue shells, immunity, traps)
   - Triggers punishments that escalate through the night
   - Updates a dramatic leaderboard with animations
3. Losing is actually fun - worse players get better items
   and the funniest punishments

GAME MODES
----------
PARTY MODE     Real pool rules + social chaos on top
TOTAL CHAOS    The app controls EVERYTHING. The table is just a prop.
SABOTEUR       One player secretly tries to lose without getting caught
LOSER'S LEAGUE Everyone tries to MISS. Vote on who's faking it.
TEAM MODE      3v3 or 4v4 with shared items and team punishments
QUICK ROUND    5-minute blitz, perfect for end of night

THE VIBE
--------
- Bad at pool? You'll have the BEST time. Blue shells target
  first place. Every miss gives you a random item. The worse
  you play, the more chaos you cause.
- Secret missions give you private objectives nobody else sees
- Punishments escalate: mild early, nuclear by last call
- Everyone submits custom punishments before the game starts
- AI trash talk commentator roasts everyone based on performance
- Post-game report card: MVP, worst shot, most embarrassing moment

WHY IT'S DIFFERENT
------------------
Pool apps today = boring training tools for serious players.
RackUp = the reason your friend group goes out every week.

Not just pool either. Darts, ping pong, beer pong, cornhole -
any bar game becomes a RackUp game.

"The Jackbox of real-world bar games."
